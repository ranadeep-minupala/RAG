================================================================================
                          CORPORATE EXPENSE POLICY
================================================================================

Policy Effective Date: January 1, 2026
Last Revised: February 6, 2026
Policy Owner: Finance Department


================================================================================
1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE
================================================================================

This Expense Policy establishes guidelines for employees to incur and claim 
business-related expenses while conducting company business. All employees, 
contractors, and consultants are expected to exercise good judgment and fiscal 
responsibility when incurring expenses on behalf of the company.

This policy applies to all business-related expenditures including travel, 
accommodation, meals, entertainment, and office supplies. The policy ensures:
- Transparency and accountability in company spending
- Fair and timely reimbursement for employees
- Compliance with tax regulations and corporate governance standards
- Prevention of fraud and financial mismanagement


================================================================================
2. GENERAL PRINCIPLES
================================================================================

All expense claims must adhere to the following core principles:

2.1 BUSINESS NECESSITY
All expenses must be ordinary and necessary for conducting company business. 
Personal expenses, even if incurred during business travel, are not reimbursable.

2.2 REASONABLENESS
Expenses should be reasonable in amount and appropriate to the circumstances. 
Employees should choose cost-effective options that balance economy with 
business needs.

2.3 COMPLIANCE
All expenses must comply with this policy and applicable laws and regulations. 
When in doubt, employees should consult with their manager or the Finance 
Department before incurring the expense.

2.4 ACCURACY
Expense reports must be accurate, complete, and submitted in a timely manner 
with all required documentation. Falsification of expense reports may result 
in disciplinary action up to and including termination.


================================================================================
3. EXPENSE CATEGORIES AND LIMITS
================================================================================

3.1 TRAVEL EXPENSES
Limit: $500 per trip
Applies to: Transportation including flights, trains, buses, taxis, and 
ride-sharing services

Guidelines:
- Book the most economical and direct mode of transportation available
- Economy class is the standard for all flights under 5 hours duration
- For flights over 5 hours, premium economy may be authorized with manager 
  approval
- Use the company's preferred travel agency or online booking tool to make 
  reservations
- Book travel at least 14 days in advance when possible to secure better rates
- Airport parking is reimbursable up to $25/day; consider alternative 
  transportation when rates exceed this amount
- Ride-sharing services (Uber, Lyft) are acceptable for business travel up to 
  $75 per trip
- Mileage reimbursement for personal vehicle use is $0.67 per mile (IRS 
  standard rate 2024), requires prior approval for trips over 100 miles

Non-Reimbursable Travel Expenses:
- Flight upgrades purchased for personal comfort
- Change fees or cancellation fees due to personal reasons
- Extra baggage fees for personal items
- Travel insurance (covered under company policy)
- In-flight entertainment, WiFi, or meals (unless required for business)
- Premium seating selections
- Commuting between home and regular workplace


3.2 ACCOMMODATION
Limit: $150 per night
Applies to: Hotel, motel, or short-term rental accommodations during business 
travel

Guidelines:
- Stay at standard or moderate hotels conveniently located near business 
  destination
- Use the company's preferred hotel booking tool or website to find best 
  rates and discounts
- Hotels must be booked through recognized platforms or chains for safety and 
  accountability
- Lodging taxes are reimbursable in addition to the nightly rate
- Extended stays (7+ nights) may qualify for corporate apartment rental with 
  Finance Director approval
- Sharing accommodations is not required but is encouraged for cost savings
- Check-out time extensions must be justified by business necessity

Non-Reimbursable Accommodation Expenses:
- Luxury or resort hotels exceeding policy limits without prior approval
- Room service charges
- Minibar expenses
- In-room movies or entertainment
- Spa, gym, or fitness center fees
- Hotel laundry or dry cleaning (unless trip exceeds 5 consecutive nights)
- Personal phone calls
- Early check-in or late check-out fees for personal convenience


3.3 MEALS AND PER DIEM
Limit: $50 per day ($55 in high-cost cities)
Applies to: Meals during business travel

Guidelines:
- Per diem covers breakfast, lunch, dinner, and incidental expenses (tips, 
  service charges)
- Receipts are required for individual meals exceeding $25
- For meals under $25, receipts are recommended but not required if total 
  daily expense is within per diem limit
- First and last day of travel: 75% of daily per diem rate ($37.50)
- Tips are limited to 20% of the pre-tax meal cost
- When meals are provided by the company, conference, or client, deduct the 
  following from your per diem:
  * Breakfast: $7.50 (15% of daily rate)
  * Lunch: $12.50 (25% of daily rate)  
  * Dinner: $20.00 (40% of daily rate)
  * Incidentals: $10.00 (20% of daily rate)
- Alcoholic beverages are not reimbursable under per diem
- Choose reasonably priced restaurants or cafés
- Fast food or quick-service meals are acceptable and cost-effective

Business Meals (Non-Travel):
- Meals with clients, partners, or vendors require manager pre-approval
- Must include names of attendees and business purpose on expense report
- Alcohol may be allowed for client entertainment up to $75 total, with 
  manager approval
- Team meals for business meetings are reimbursable with department head 
  approval


3.4 ENTERTAINMENT AND CLIENT HOSTING
Limit: $100 per event
Applies to: Business entertainment including client dinners, conference 
attendance, and professional events

Guidelines:
- Entertainment expenses must serve a clear business purpose
- Obtain prior approval from manager or supervisor for all entertainment 
  expenses over $50
- Provide detailed description of:
  * Event name and date
  * Attendees (names, titles, company affiliations)
  * Business objective or expected outcome
  * Itemized receipt showing what was purchased
- Conference registration fees are reimbursable with manager approval
- Networking events and industry seminars qualify as business entertainment
- Sporting events, concerts, or shows require VP-level approval and must 
  demonstrate clear business value
- Gratuities up to 20% of pre-tax amount are reimbursable

Non-Reimbursable Entertainment:
- Personal entertainment (movies, recreational activities)
- Social club memberships
- Gifts for clients (covered under separate gift policy, $100 annual limit)
- Entertainment for employee morale without clear business justification
- Gambling or gaming expenses
- Adult entertainment venues


3.5 OFFICE SUPPLIES AND EQUIPMENT
Limit: $25 per month (individual purchases)
Applies to: Work-related supplies and minor equipment

Guidelines:
- Office supplies must be necessary for work duties
- Use company's approved vendors or suppliers when possible
- Allowable items include:
  * Stationery, pens, notebooks, folders
  * Printer paper and toner/ink cartridges
  * Postage and shipping supplies
  * Basic desk accessories
  * Batteries for work equipment
- Equipment purchases over $100 require IT or Operations approval
- Software subscriptions require IT approval regardless of cost
- Submit itemized receipts for all office supply purchases

Non-Reimbursable Office Items:
- Personal items (magazines, books not related to work)
- Decorative items or plants
- Personal electronics or accessories
- Ergonomic equipment (handled through HR/facilities request process)
- Gifts for colleagues
- Coffee, snacks, or food items


3.6 COMMUNICATION EXPENSES
Limit: Varies by role
Applies to: Business-related phone, internet, and communication costs

Guidelines:
- Mobile phone reimbursement: $50/month for employees required to use 
  personal phones for business
- International calls must be work-related and pre-approved for amounts over 
  $25
- Internet expenses during travel are reimbursable when required for work
- Video conferencing tool subscriptions require manager approval
- Data roaming charges while traveling internationally are reimbursable with 
  itemized billing


3.7 PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT
Limit: Varies by department budget
Applies to: Training, certifications, conferences, professional memberships

Guidelines:
- Professional development expenses require manager approval before commitment
- Reimbursable items include:
  * Conference registration fees
  * Professional certification exam fees
  * Required training materials and textbooks
  * Professional association annual dues
- Include business justification and expected benefit
- Certificates or receipts of completion must be provided for training
- Industry conferences limited to 2 per employee per year without VP approval

4. EXPENSE SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS
================================================================================

4.1 DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
To ensure timely and accurate reimbursement, all expense claims must include:

Required for All Expenses Over $25:
- Original itemized receipts (digital photos or scans acceptable)
- Receipts must clearly show:
  * Date of purchase
  * Vendor/merchant name and location
  * Itemized list of goods/services purchased
  * Total amount paid
  * Form of payment
- Credit card statements alone are NOT acceptable as proof

Special Documentation Requirements:
- Meals with clients: Include attendee names, company affiliations, and 
  business purpose
- Mileage claims: Provide starting point, destination, purpose, and odometer 
  readings
- Conference attendance: Submit conference agenda or program
- Entertainment: Detailed explanation of business objective and outcomes

Lost Receipt Procedure:
- Submit a Lost Receipt Affidavit (available from Finance Department)
- Include explanation of expense and circumstances of lost receipt
- Manager approval required for lost receipts over $50
- Maximum of 3 lost receipt affidavits per quarter per employee


4.2 SUBMISSION TIMEFRAME
Timely submission ensures accurate bookkeeping and prompt reimbursement.

Standard Submission Deadline:
- All expenses must be submitted within 30 calendar days of being incurred
- Corporate credit card expenses must be reconciled within 5 business days of 
  statement receipt
- Monthly recurring expenses (mobile phone, subscriptions) submit by 5th of 
  following month

Late Submission Policy:
- Expenses submitted 31-60 days after incurrence require manager approval
- Expenses submitted 61-90 days after incurrence require department head 
  approval with written justification
- Expenses over 90 days old will NOT be reimbursed without Finance Director 
  written approval
- Year-end cutoff: All expenses for current fiscal year must be submitted by 
  last business day of fiscal year (no exceptions)

Special Circumstances:
- Extended illness or leave: Contact Finance Department for exception process
- System outages: Deadline extended by number of days system was unavailable


4.3 APPROVAL PROCESS
Multi-level approval ensures proper oversight and budget control.

Approval Thresholds:
- Up to $500: Requires direct manager approval
  * Turnaround: 3 business days
  
- $501 to $2,000: Requires department head approval
  * Turnaround: 5 business days
  * Must include business justification
  
- $2,001 to $5,000: Requires Finance Director approval
  * Turnaround: 7 business days
  * Must include detailed business case
  
- Over $5,000: Requires CFO approval
  * Turnaround: 10 business days
  * Must include competitive quotes or justification for vendor selection

Pre-Approval Requirements:
The following expenses must be pre-approved BEFORE being incurred:
- Any single expense over $500
- Entertainment expenses over $50
- International travel
- Out-of-policy expenses (exceptions to standard limits)
- Conference attendance requiring travel
- Professional development over $500

Managers' Responsibilities:
- Review expense reports within 2 business days of submission
- Verify expenses are reasonable, necessary, and within policy
- Ensure proper documentation is attached
- Question any unusual or out-of-policy expenses
- Provide clear feedback when rejecting expenses


4.4 EXPENSE REPORT SUBMISSION PROCESS
Step 1: Gather all receipts and documentation
Step 2: Complete expense report form (available on company intranet or expense 
        management system)
Step 3: Categorize each expense correctly
Step 4: Attach all supporting documentation
Step 5: Add business purpose for each line item
Step 6: Submit for manager approval
Step 7: Track reimbursement status in expense management system

5. NON-REIMBURSABLE EXPENSES
================================================================================

The following expenses are NOT eligible for reimbursement under any 
circumstances:

5.1 PERSONAL EXPENSES
- Personal grooming services (haircuts, spa treatments)
- Personal toiletries and hygiene products
- Personal clothing purchases (except safety equipment approved by manager)
- Personal medical expenses or prescriptions
- Personal insurance premiums
- Gym memberships or fitness classes
- Personal legal fees

5.2 COMMUTING AND TRANSPORTATION
- Regular commute between home and primary workplace
- Parking at primary workplace (unless client visit from office)
- Personal vehicle maintenance or repairs
- Personal vehicle insurance
- Traffic violations, parking tickets, or other fines

5.3 FAMILY AND DEPENDENT CARE
- Childcare or daycare expenses
- Pet care or boarding
- Family members' travel expenses (unless approved for relocation)
- Spouse or partner travel (except approved spouse travel policy)

5.4 PENALTIES AND VIOLATIONS
- Late fees on personal credit cards
- Overdraft fees or bank charges
- Traffic tickets, parking violations, or speeding fines
- Court fees or legal penalties
- Lost baggage replacement (should file claim with airline)

5.5 LOSSES AND THEFT
- Lost cash or personal property
- Stolen personal items (file police report and insurance claim)
- Lost or stolen personal credit cards
- Damage to personal property

5.6 UNAUTHORIZED EXPENSES
- Expenses incurred without proper pre-approval when required
- Expenses that violate company ethics policy
- Political contributions or donations
- Personal charitable donations (company has separate donation matching program)

6. REIMBURSEMENT PROCESS
================================================================================

6.1 PAYMENT TIMELINE AND METHODS
The company is committed to prompt reimbursement for legitimate business 
expenses.

Standard Reimbursement Timeline:
- Properly submitted expenses: Reimbursed within 14 business days of approval
- Complex or high-value expenses: Up to 21 business days
- Expenses requiring additional documentation: Timeline pauses until received

Payment Methods:
- Primary method: Direct deposit to employee's designated bank account on file
- Payment processed during regular payroll cycle (bi-weekly)
- International employees: Wire transfer within 21 business days
- Emergency reimbursement: Available for hardship cases with VP approval, 
  processed within 3 business days

Tracking Your Reimbursement:
- Log into expense management system to check status
- Email notifications sent at each stage: Submitted → Approved → Paid
- For questions, contact expense@company.com with report number


6.2 CORPORATE CREDIT CARDS
Company-issued cards streamline the expense process and protect personal 
finances.

Eligibility:
- Employees who travel regularly (3+ trips per year)
- Managers and above
- Department heads who regularly incur business expenses
- Sales team members

Corporate Card Requirements:
- Use exclusively for business purposes (personal use is strictly prohibited)
- Reconcile statements monthly within 5 business days of receipt
- Submit itemized receipts for all transactions
- Report lost or stolen cards immediately (24/7 hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX)
- Card will be deactivated upon employment termination

Personal Charges on Corporate Card:
- Accidental personal charges must be reported immediately
- Reimburse company within 10 business days via payroll deduction or check
- Repeated personal use may result in card revocation and disciplinary action
- Intentional personal use is considered theft and grounds for termination

Card Limits:
- Standard employee card: $5,000/month
- Manager card: $10,000/month  
- Director card: $25,000/month
- Limit increases require Finance Director approval


6.3 PERSONAL REIMBURSEMENT (OUT-OF-POCKET)
For employees without corporate cards or for approved personal expenses.

Best Practices:
- Use personal credit cards rather than cash when possible for better tracking
- Photograph receipts immediately to prevent loss
- Keep digital copies of all receipts as backup
- Submit expenses at least weekly rather than waiting until month-end
- For large purchases, request advance payment when possible

Advance Payment Requests:
- Available for anticipated expenses over $1,000
- Must be requested at least 10 business days before expense
- Requires itemized estimate and business justification
- Unused advance must be returned within 5 business days of trip completion
- Failure to return or reconcile advance may result in payroll deduction


================================================================================
7. TRAVEL ADVANCES AND FOREIGN CURRENCY
================================================================================

7.1 TRAVEL ADVANCES
Travel advances help employees manage cash flow during expensive business 
trips.

Advance Eligibility:
- Business trips expected to exceed $1,000 in out-of-pocket expenses
- International travel requiring currency exchange
- Extended travel (5+ consecutive nights)
- New employees without established expense history

How to Request an Advance:
- Submit Travel Advance Request Form at least 10 business days before departure
- Include detailed itinerary and estimated expenses
- Requires manager approval
- Payment issued 3-5 business days before departure
- Maximum advance: 80% of estimated total expenses, up to $3,000

Reconciling Your Advance:
- Submit final expense report within 5 business days of return
- Attach all receipts and documentation
- Advance will be reconciled against actual expenses
- Surplus must be returned immediately
- Shortfall will be reimbursed per standard timeline
- Failure to reconcile within 30 days may result in payroll deduction and 
  ineligibility for future advances


7.2 FOREIGN CURRENCY AND INTERNATIONAL EXPENSES
International business travel requires special considerations.

Currency Conversion:
- Expenses will be reimbursed based on exchange rate on date of transaction
- Attach credit card statement showing converted amount, OR
- Use company's approved currency conversion tool (XE.com or OANDA)
- Include screenshot of exchange rate used
- Foreign transaction fees (typically 3%) are reimbursable

International Per Diem Rates:
- Rates vary by country and city
- Refer to U.S. Department of State per diem rates for international locations
- High-cost cities (London, Tokyo, Zurich): Up to $100/day for meals
- Standard international locations: Up to $75/day for meals
- Lodging rates vary significantly by location

International Travel Additional Guidelines:
- Passport and visa fees are reimbursable for business travel
- Business class approved for flights over 8 hours with VP approval
- Travel vaccinations required for business travel are reimbursable
- International mobile phone plans or SIM cards are reimbursable
- Airport lounge access for flights over 6 hours is reimbursable up to $50
- Travel insurance may be provided by company for international trips

8. POLICY COMPLIANCE AND VIOLATIONS
================================================================================

8.1 CONSEQUENCES OF POLICY VIOLATIONS
The company takes expense policy compliance seriously. Violations undermine 
trust and can create legal and financial risks.

Types of Violations:
- Submitting false or inflated expense claims
- Claiming personal expenses as business expenses  
- Failing to return excess advances or overpayments
- Using corporate credit card for personal expenses
- Submitting duplicate expense claims
- Failing to provide required documentation
- Bypassing approval processes
- Repeated failure to comply with submission deadlines

Progressive Disciplinary Action:
First Offense (Minor):
- Verbal warning from manager
- Mandatory policy review and acknowledgment
- Expense report rejected until corrected

Second Offense or First Major Violation:
- Written warning placed in personnel file
- Mandatory meeting with HR and Finance
- Possible suspension of corporate card privileges
- Required pre-approval for all expenses for 6 months
- May require repayment of improper expenses

Third Offense or Serious Violation:
- Final written warning
- Suspension without pay (1-5 days depending on severity)
- Permanent revocation of corporate card
- All expenses require VP approval for 12 months
- Repayment of all improper expenses

Termination-Level Violations:
- Fraudulent expense claims (falsifying receipts, claiming fictitious expenses)
- Intentional repeated violations after final warning
- Expense fraud exceeding $500
- Using corporate card for cash advances
- Conspiracy with vendors to inflate expenses

Recovery of Improper Payments:
- Company reserves right to recover improperly claimed expenses
- Recovery methods include:
  * Voluntary repayment
  * Payroll deduction
  * Legal action if necessary
- Interest may be charged on amounts over $1,000 not repaid within 30 days


8.2 REPORTING SUSPECTED VIOLATIONS
All employees have a responsibility to maintain integrity in the expense 
process.

How to Report:
- Speak directly with your manager if comfortable
- Contact HR confidentially: hr@company.com or 1-800-XXX-XXXX
- Use anonymous ethics hotline: 1-888-XXX-XXXX
- Submit written report to Ethics Officer

Protections:
- No retaliation for good-faith reports of policy violations
- Anonymous reporting available
- Whistleblower protection under company policy and applicable law


8.3 MANAGER RESPONSIBILITIES
Managers play a critical role in ensuring policy compliance.

Manager Duties:
- Review and approve/reject expense reports promptly
- Verify expenses are reasonable and necessary
- Ensure team members understand policy requirements
- Address violations promptly and consistently  
- Model appropriate expense behavior
- Conduct annual policy refresher training with team
- Monitor spending trends and escalate concerns

Managers Who Approve Improper Expenses:
- May be held accountable for pattern of poor oversight
- Required to complete additional training
- May impact performance review

9. SPECIAL CIRCUMSTANCES AND EXCEPTIONS
================================================================================

9.1 TEMPORARY DUTY ASSIGNMENTS
Extended assignments (30+ days) in a single location.

Special Provisions:
- Lodging: Corporate housing or extended-stay hotel rates negotiable
- Meals: Per diem reduced to 75% after first 30 days
- Transportation: Rental car approved for assignments over 60 days
- Periodic home visits: One trip home per month reimbursed for assignments 
  over 90 days

9.2 RELOCATION EXPENSES
Approved relocations for business purposes.

Covered Under Separate Relocation Policy:
- Moving expenses
- Temporary housing
- Home sale assistance
- Spouse job search assistance
- Refer to HR for full relocation policy


9.3 REMOTE WORK AND HOME OFFICE
Employees working primarily from home.

Reimbursable Home Office Expenses:
- Internet service: Up to $50/month with manager approval
- Office supplies: Standard $25/month policy applies
- Ergonomic equipment: Handled through separate facilities request process
- Not reimbursable: Rent, utilities, home maintenance


9.4 EMERGENCY SITUATIONS
Unexpected circumstances requiring immediate action.

Emergency Expense Guidelines:
- Personal safety and security take precedence over policy limits
- Document circumstances thoroughly
- Notify manager as soon as safely possible
- Submit detailed expense report with explanation
- Examples: Emergency medical care, evacuation due to natural disaster, 
  unplanned overnight stay due to travel disruption


9.5 POLICY EXCEPTIONS
Occasionally, legitimate business needs may require exception to policy limits.

Exception Request Process:
- Submit Exception Request Form with detailed justification
- Include cost-benefit analysis when relevant
- Approval requirements:
  * Up to 50% over limit: Department head approval
  * 51-100% over limit: Finance Director approval  
  * Over 100% of limit: CFO approval
- Exceptions are not precedent-setting
- Frequent exception requests may trigger policy review

10. ADDITIONAL POLICIES AND GUIDELINES
================================================================================

10.1 SUSTAINABILITY AND ENVIRONMENTAL RESPONSIBILITY
The company encourages environmentally responsible travel choices.

Sustainable Travel Practices:
- Choose direct flights when possible to reduce emissions
- Use public transportation when practical and safe
- Consider video conferencing as alternative to travel when appropriate
- Choose hotels with environmental certifications when available
- Decline single-use plastics and excessive packaging
- Use digital receipts when available to reduce paper waste


10.2 HEALTH AND SAFETY
Employee wellbeing is paramount during business travel.

Health and Safety Requirements:
- Follow all company safety protocols during travel
- Check travel advisories before international travel
- Register with company's travel safety system
- Maintain travel insurance coverage through company plan
- Report any safety incidents or concerns immediately
- Do not travel to restricted or high-risk areas without security approval


10.3 ETHICS AND ANTI-CORRUPTION
Expense practices must align with company ethics standards.

Ethical Guidelines:
- Never accept or provide kickbacks or bribes
- Decline gifts from vendors that could create conflict of interest
- Entertainment of government officials requires special approval and 
  compliance review
- All expenses must be accurately recorded (no off-books arrangements)
- Comply with Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and local anti-corruption 
  laws

What Auditors Review:
- Compliance with policy limits and guidelines

Employee Responsibilities During Audit:
